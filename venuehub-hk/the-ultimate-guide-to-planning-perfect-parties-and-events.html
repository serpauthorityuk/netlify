The Ultimate Guide to Planning Perfect Parties and Events

There is a lot at stake when you are responsible for planning parties and events. When things go well, a party will be a huge success and guests will be happy with the event and are likely to choose the same party planning service again as they will have fostered trust between supplier and client.

The services of party planners and event organisers are often quite high as they bring a huge amount of expertise to the table, and are adept at creating events which are truly special and memorable for all the right reasons.

As every single party is different, planners need to spend a considerable amount of time getting to know their clients so they can put together a plan that really resonates with the client. Not every party requires the services of a planner, and it is absolutely possible to plan and pull off a wonderful party with limited experience.

The first step in planning a party is to set a budget. Your chosen budget will have a strong bearing on the direction your event takes including event room hire and even the menus, so it is worth doing some research into the general costs of the things you will need to help you along the way. Above all, a budget needs to be realistic and it should be adhered to as closely as possible to avoid spiralling costs.

Choosing a theme for your own party is pretty easy - you happen to know exactly what you like and don’t like. Planning a party theme for someone else is a very different thing. You should spend time getting to know what the client needs from their party, and this is often deeply personal to them. Consult with them frequently to make sure you are heading in the right direction and to give them plenty of time to give feedback with enough time to make changes if needed.

Planning a corporate party tends to be a less personal affair. Themes are usually limited to a corporate style which suits the look and feel of the host company and should always be professional and polished. While having a well defined corporate identity can help to simplify the process of choosing a theme, brands can often be very firm and inflexible on what they want which can complicate things for planners so you might need to prepare for some pushback when choosing venue hire in Hong Kong and elsewhere.

Settling on a date for an event can be simple if there is a particular holiday or other annual occasion which takes place at a predictable time each year. For events which don’t have a set date requirement you should think about roughly when you would like it to take place in the year. Choosing a date can be difficult, and the time of year and even the day of the week can limit or expand your choices and affect your budget.

Some venues will charge more for events hosted during peak times. Always be sure to check with your venue and ask them for an upfront cost for the day of the week and time of year you are considering. Summer and the festive season are often considered peak times of the year, and weekends are usually considered peak days of the week. Events on Sundays and earlier on in the week can be cheaper at times so ask for the specifics from your party room hire provider.

Your venue choice is vital and can make or break your event in terms of success and satisfaction. Match the venue to the feel you want to achieve with your event and do your research. Many venues will have a portfolio that you can use to view previous events they have hosted which will give you a good idea of the kind of party you can expect to throw there.

Whichever venue you choose, make sure it is the right size for your chosen guest list. There is nothing worse than hosting an intimate event in a space which is too large and feels awkward for attendees. The same can be said for not booking a large enough venue, though this can prevent your party from taking place at all.
